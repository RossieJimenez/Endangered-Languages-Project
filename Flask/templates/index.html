Voices at Risk: Exploring the Crisis of Endangered Languages

Language, a cornerstone of culture and identity, faces unprecedented threats today. In our digital era, globalization has propelled a select few languages to dominance, leaving countless others teetering on the edge of extinction. However, beyond mere statistics, lies a tapestry woven with rich cultural heritage, traditions, and knowledge systems on the brink of obscurity. Endangerment Classification Key

The classification is not based on the number of speakers left. To understand the level of endangerment for each language, please use the following key classifications:

  • Vulnerable: Most children speak the language but may restrict its use to certain settings, like home.
  • Definitely Endangered: Children no longer learn the language as their primary tongue at home.
  • Severely Endangered: Spoken by older generations; parents may understand but don't pass it on.
  • Critically Endangered: Youngest speakers are grandparents; language is rarely spoken.
  • Extinct: No speakers remain.

Additional Information

The United Nations Education, Scientific and Cultural Organisation (UNESCO) regularly publishes a list of endangered languages, using a classification system that describes its danger (or completion) of extinction. Please explore the website to learn more!
